Resources

This page is dedicated to providing and showcasing resources and programs designed for educators of color in every stage of their journey. Here, you’ll find mentorship opportunities, scholarship programs, helpful hiring practices, on campus clubs, and links to organizations advocating for equity in education. These resources aim to empower, inspire, and help you thrive in your career.

The Teachers of Color Foundation

The Teachers of Color Foundation is a non profit organization that's goal is to improve and increase the diversity of teachers in a certain district.This Foundation was founded by students who saw the major ike of diversity and wanted to make that change happen. They have also created a scholarship which awards students who plan on becoming teachers of color. The benefits for students include Higher graduation rates, Proportional discipline, Culturally responsive classrooms, Improved family engagement, Higher college acceptance rates, Diversity in educational leadership.

The National Council of Teachers of English

The Cultivating New Voices among Scholars of Color (CNV) program by NCTE supports early-career scholars of color with mentoring, networking, and a $1,200 research stipend. It encourages culturally grounded research in language, literacy, and education. The program is open to U.S.-based doctoral candidates or recent graduates, participants engage in a two-year cohort with mentorship and professional development opportunities. .

EduColor

The Educolor is a nationwide community that supports developing resources for teachers of color. Educolor engage teachers online or offline to speak about their goal and prove resources on how to elevate topics in school about equity and justice. This foundation was formed in 2014 by people of color for people of color to best help educators to receive the help or mentor needed.

The Education Trust

The Education Trust-West and California County Superintendents Educational Services Association, placed significant importance on the pivotal role that education stakeholders such as administrators, board members, staff, etc.. play in a child’s school experience. In order for students to be able to perform well academically and be culturally aware, schools must be racially diverse. Because of that, schools must fix the way that they are recruiting and retaining their teachers of color. Recruitment and Retaining tips are included in the document for school personnel to follow. Further, the document encourages education stakeholders to take action on the issue of recruiting, hiring, and retaining teachers of color by starting a conversation.

iChange Collaborative

The I Change Collaborative works to support educational change by empowering individuals and communities to create inclusive, equitable, and culturally responsive systems. They provide a platform for professional development, leadership, and advocacy aimed at improving the education of marginalized communities

Center for Racial Equity in Education

The Center for Racial Equity in Education (CREED) focuses on addressing racial inequities within the North Carolina education system. Its work includes advocating for policy changes, providing research-based recommendations, and equipping educators with the tools needed to support students of color. CREED's initiatives aim to ensure that students of color are central to educational practices and receive the equitable opportunities promised by the 1954 Brown v. Board of Education ruling.

The California Association for Asian Pacific American Education

The California Association for Asian Pacific American Education (CAAPAE) works to advocate for the educational needs of Asian Pacific American (APA) students, teachers, and educators. Founded in 1994, the organization focuses on increasing public awareness, promoting the inclusion of APA history and culture in curricula, and providing professional development opportunities. CAAPAE also supports research and leadership development within the community.

The National Alliance of Black School Educators

The National Alliance of Black School Educators (NABSE) is a key organization dedicated to improving the academic success of children, particularly those of African descent. It provides resources for educators, advocates for policy change, and creates programs focused on leadership development, student achievement, and educator recognition. NABSE emphasizes the importance of a transformative educational experience for both educators and students, aiming for a sustainable and impactful future.

The National Association for Multicultural Education

The National Association for Multicultural Education (NAME) is a leading organization focused on promoting equity, inclusion, and social justice in education. Established in 1990, NAME works towards diversifying education by providing resources and advocating for multicultural education. The organization encourages participation from educators, policymakers, and community members to ensure educational systems are inclusive and culturally responsive. It offers conferences, professional development resources, and a journal, Multicultural Perspectives.

Caminantes for Education

This page represents a student-run organization dedicated to supporting and uplifting Latinx and other students of color who are passionate about pursuing a career in the field of education. Their mission is to create a welcoming and inclusive space where students can connect, share experiences, and receive guidance as they navigate their academic and professional journeys. The group strives to empower new members by offering resources, mentorship, and opportunities that encourage personal and professional growth, ultimately helping them achieve their goals in education and beyond.

The Asian American Education Project

The Asian American Education Project provides resources and professional development aimed at promoting Asian American history and culture in schools. It offers curriculum materials, lesson plans, and thematic units that educators can use to teach students about the contributions and experiences of Asian Americans. The organization also hosts workshops and webinars to help educators integrate these topics into their classrooms.

DonorsChoose

DonorsChoose is a nonprofit platform that allows teachers to post classroom project needs, enabling donors to contribute directly to fund these requests. Projects often include classroom supplies, technology, books, and innovative learning tools. This platform helps bridge the gap in resources for underserved schools and supports educators in creating dynamic learning environments for students.
